In participants with dyslexia, Reader View did not produce a statistically significant speed improvement (Welch t-test p=0.697, log-speed t-test p=0.908, OLS-adjusted p=0.619). The observed mean difference in raw speed was -18.94 (Reader View minus control), and the OLS reader_view coefficient was 0.0293. The reader_view*dyslexia interaction was not significant (p=0.661), and interpretable models did not identify a strong consistent reader_view effect. Therefore evidence does not support a clear improvement in reading speed for individuals with dyslexia."}
